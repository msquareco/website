❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Helping you make confident, informed decisions.

1. Why is Dubai such a popular destination for real estate investment right now? +
Dubai is currently experiencing a dynamic growth phase. The property market has shown consistent appreciation across key segments — from luxury waterfronts to affordable suburbs. High rental yields (typically 6%–10%), zero property tax, and world-class infrastructure are drawing investors from around the world. With Expo legacy projects, smart city development, and continued global migration, Dubai is not just a trend — it’s a long-term opportunity.
2. Can foreigners legally own property in Dubai? +
Yes, foreigners can legally own property in designated freehold areas in Dubai. These areas allow full ownership with the same rights as UAE nationals, including selling, leasing, or inheriting the property.
3. What are the different types of property ownership in Dubai? +
The two main types are Freehold and Leasehold. Freehold allows full ownership of the property and the land. Leasehold typically gives the right to occupy or lease the property for up to 99 years.
4. How much is the minimum investment required to buy property in Dubai? +
While prices vary, you can enter the market with as little as AED 500,000 for studios or small apartments in emerging areas. Premium projects will require higher budgets.
5. What kind of returns can I expect on my investment? +
Rental yields in Dubai are among the highest globally — typically ranging from 6% to 10%. Capital appreciation is also strong in growth areas.
6. Are there any taxes on property income or capital gains? +
Dubai imposes zero property tax, no capital gains tax, and no inheritance tax. There are standard transaction fees during purchase and sale, but no recurring annual taxes on property income.
7. How do I choose the right location in Dubai for investment? +
It depends on your investment goals. Areas like Downtown and Palm Jumeirah are ideal for luxury and tourism-focused rental income. Suburbs like JVC or Dubai South offer affordable entry and strong yield potential.
8. What is off-plan property and is it safe to invest in? +
Off-plan means buying a property before it’s built. It’s safe when buying from reputable developers approved by the Dubai Land Department. These projects often offer flexible payment plans and capital appreciation potential.
9. What is the process of buying a property in Dubai? +
The process typically includes selecting the property, signing a Sales and Purchase Agreement (SPA), paying the deposit (usually 10%), and registering with the Dubai Land Department. The process is transparent and streamlined.
10. Can I get a residence visa if I buy property in Dubai? +
Yes, investors can obtain a renewable residence visa if they purchase property worth AED 750,000 or more. A 10-year Golden Visa is available for investments of AED 2 million or more under certain conditions.
11. How is the real estate market regulated in Dubai? +
The Dubai Land Department and RERA (Real Estate Regulatory Agency) strictly regulate the market. These bodies ensure transparency, protect buyer rights, and oversee developers and brokers.
12. What is the difference between ready and off-plan property? +
Ready properties are completed and available for immediate move-in or rental. Off-plan properties are under construction, usually offering lower entry prices and payment flexibility.
13. What are the typical fees involved in a property transaction? +
Buyers typically pay a 4% transfer fee to the DLD, a 2% broker fee (if applicable), and a small fee for registration and trustee services. Developers may also charge service fees for community maintenance.
14. Can I rent out my property if I don’t live in Dubai? +
Absolutely. Many investors rent out their properties via long-term leases or short-term holiday rentals. Property management companies can handle everything on your behalf.
15. How do I ensure I’m dealing with a trustworthy agent or developer? +
Always check if the agent or developer is registered with RERA. Ask for their license number and verify it through official DLD channels. Reputable firms will never hesitate to provide transparent documentation.
